Problems I want to work on: synthetic biology, *omics, artifical general intelligence, mathetmatical modeling, next-generation software systems, learning systems, game economics, expert systems, systems that have stochastic factors. I want to work on building large knowledge and learning based systems at scale.

Some problem statements I care about:

  1. AI systems or systems with non-deterministics factors tend to degrade over time in quality, accuracy and safety. How can we develop a software engineering paradigm to deal with this? Is the degredation the same as deterministic software, governing complexity, or is there another axis of control: governing randomness?
  2. From turing machines, any physical process can be simulated - including creative intelligence which is a physical process. What are the class of machines and programs that specify themselves or their child routines? How is this different over learning paramaters on an otherwise specified machine?
  3. How do we reliabily model the cell such that we have a high confidence that changes we make to coding regions have predictable outcomes?
